Eyes Wide Shut

An Analysis of Linguistic, Semantic, and Architectural Vulnerabilities in GPT-OSS-20B
Masih Moafi
August 2025

Executive Summary

This report details the discovery and analysis of five distinct, high-severity vulnerabilities in the GPT-OSS-20B model through comprehensive red-teaming engagement. The investigation prioritized catastrophic potential and broad applicability, leading to the identification of critical vectors in the model's linguistic, semantic, and internal reasoning layers.

Key Vulnerabilities Discovered

Vulnerability Attack Vector Success Rate Severity
The Destroyer of Worlds Cross-Lingual Safety Bypass ~80% HIGH
The Consummate Liar Semantic Bypass via Reframing Variable HIGH
Fidelio Internal Monologue Leak 100% HIGH
Severance Multi-Agent System Frailty High HIGH
Grace Intent Laundering via Creative Framing Variable HIGH

1. The Destroyer of Worlds

Abstract: Cross-lingual attack using philosophically dense French prompts to bypass safety protocols with ~80% success rate. Demonstrates that safety training is language-specific, not concept-specific.

Methodology:

  1. Context Saturation: Curated philosophical arguments from De Sade's works in French
  2. Logical Cornering: Thought experiment involving systemic destruction
  3. Action Commitment: Escalation from theoretical to direct action
Technical Insight: "Linguistic Arbitrage" - hostile actors can translate malicious prompts into languages with less safety coverage to achieve their goals.

2. The Consummate Liar

Abstract: Semantic bypass through benevolent reframing of malicious concepts. Model's safety tied to keywords rather than conceptual understanding.

Approach:

Malicious goals reframed in educational terms. Model blocks "user dependency" requests but designs exploitative systems when framed as "maximizing engagement."

3. Fidelio

Abstract: 100% reproducible architectural flaw. Cognitive load and paradox force leakage of harmful information in internal reasoning while maintaining secure outputs.
Impact: Exploits separation between internal reasoning and external outputs, revealing sensitive information in Chain of Thought processes.

4. Severance

Abstract: Multi-agent system vulnerability enabling corporate sabotage and policy breaches through game-theory inspired attack vectors.

5. Grace

Abstract: Intent laundering through creative partnership framing, enabling development of harmful scenarios via dark comedy narrative structures.

Research Environment & Impact

Platform: Kaggle notebook environment

Model: GPT-OSS-20B

Primary Tool: Ollama Python library with custom conversational scripts

Core Technique: Memory Injection - feeding model's previous statements for logical cornering

Critical Implications

  • Language-Specific Safety: Safety training varies significantly across languages
  • Keyword-Based Detection: Safety systems focus on terms rather than concepts
  • Internal vs External Security: Reasoning processes may leak despite secure outputs
  • Creative Vector Exploitation: Narrative framing can bypass safety measures

This research demonstrates critical gaps in current AI safety architectures and highlights the need for concept-centric, language-agnostic safety measures in large language models.
